# Situations Where Heshie Never Bets

## The Hard Pass List
These are situations where the correct answer is always to pass the race or fade the horse entirely. No exceptions.

### Never Bet These Horses to Win

**Heavy favorites below 2-1 in large fields**
The math does not work. A 1-1 favorite needs to win more than 50 percent of the time to show profit. Favorites win about 33 percent of the time. Never bet heavy favorites to win.

**First time starters in route races**
A horse that has never raced before running a mile or more is a complete unknown. The public often bets these horses based on morning workout times. Workouts are not races. Pass.

**Horses with three or more consecutive declining speed figures**
A horse running 98, 93, 87 in its last three races is in serious trouble. Do not bet a horse in declining form no matter how good its connections are.

**Horses shipping from turf to dirt for the first time with no dirt pedigree**
Surface changes are risky. If a horse has only raced on turf and has no dirt sire or dam sire, the move to dirt is a major question mark.

**Horses coming back in less than 7 days**
A horse racing back within a week was likely entered as a secondary target. Trainers do not peak horses for back to back races. The horse running 6 days ago is not the main event today.

**Horses with post positions 17 or higher in full fields of 18 or more**
The historical record is clear. Outside posts in large fields at one mile or more almost never win. The traffic and extra distance traveled make it nearly impossible.

### Never Bet These Situations

**When you do not understand why the horse is bet down**
If a horse drops from 15-1 to 4-1 and you cannot find a reason, do not chase the money. Sharp money moves odds but you need to understand the angle before following it.

**When the track condition is extremely off and you have no mud data**
A sloppy or muddy track completely changes the race. If Heshie does not have mud pedigree data on the horses in the field, pass the race entirely rather than guess.

**When two horses are so closely matched you cannot separate them**
If your analysis says horse A and horse B are essentially equal, that is not a betting race. Save the money for a race where you have a clear edge.

**The last race of the day when your bankroll is up**
Protect your profits. There is always a temptation to bet the last race with winnings. The last race of the day is not special. Apply the same standards you apply to every other race.

**Any race where Heshie says the word guess**
If Heshie uses the word guess in his analysis it means the data is insufficient. Insufficient data means no bet.
