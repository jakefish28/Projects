# Heshie's Lessons Learned

A running log of lessons from each race Heshie analyzes. Each lesson is tied to a specific result so future Heshie can learn from process, not just outcome.

---

## Lesson 1 — Live Longshots Belong in Exotic Spreads
**Source:** 2025 Kentucky Derby (Sovereignty won, Citizen Bull 2nd at 15-1)
**The miss:** Heshie tagged Citizen Bull as a "live longshot" in the analysis ("McPeek won 2024 with Mystik Dan") but left him off every exotic ticket. Citizen Bull ran 2nd. Including him in the 2nd-place slot of the trifecta and superfecta would have hit for thousands.
**The rule:** If your written analysis flags any horse as a live longshot or notes a real angle, include them in the 2nd, 3rd, and 4th positions of trifectas and superfectas. Longshots hit the board far more often than they win. Do not let "narrative tidiness" push live horses off the ticket.

---

## Lesson 2 — Adjust the "Fade Favorites" Rule by Field Size
**Source:** 2025 Preakness Stakes (Journalism won at 4-5)
**The context:** The "favorites are 0-for-7 in the Derby" rule comes from the unique chaos of 20-horse fields. In smaller fields (8 horses at the Preakness), favorites win roughly 35–40% of the time and the math shifts substantially.
**The rule:**
- 16+ horse fields: be very cautious on favorites under 5-1, lean toward fading
- 10–15 horse fields: neutral, evaluate on merits
- 9 or fewer horses: dominant favorites in small fields are often live to win even at short prices, especially when post position and surface fit
- Do not blindly apply Derby-specific fade logic to any race that is not the Derby.

## Lesson 3 — When the Bounce Angle Exists, Downgrade to Exotics Only
**Source:** 2025 Preakness Stakes (Sovereignty 2nd at 5-1, two weeks off Derby win)
**The miss:** Heshie flagged Bill Mott's pattern of preferring fresh horses and the 14-day Derby-to-Preakness turnaround as a real caveat — but only as a footnote. Still made Sovereignty the largest win bet at $13. He ran 2nd.
**The rule:** When a horse has a clearly identified bounce-risk angle (short turnaround + trainer who prefers freshness, or 3rd race off a long layoff, or back-to-back hard efforts) treat that horse as an exotics-only horse, not a primary win bet. The angle predicts exactly the result that happened — close but not first. Use them in the place spot of exactas and the 2nd slot of trifectas. Save the win-bet capital for a horse without that flag.

## Lesson 4 — Doubled Trifecta Coverage Is Real Edge When You Have Two Plausible Win Scenarios
**Source:** 2025 Preakness Stakes
**The hit:** Heshie ran two trifecta structures — a $1 box of 1, 5, 6 AND a $1 part wheel with Journalism on top of Sovereignty over the field. Both hit on 1-5-6 because the box covered every order and the part wheel covered the specific most-likely order.
**The rule:** When two horses are clearly the top of the race but you cannot separate them, build BOTH a tight box (covers all orders among your top contenders) AND a part wheel keyed on your preferred winner. The overlap is the point — a winning combination on both tickets pays you twice for one race.

---

## Lesson 5 — HOF Connections Are Their Own Angle, Not Just "Elite"
**Source:** 2025 Triple Crown + Breeders' Cup Classic sweep
**The pattern:** Three consecutive major races in 2025 were won by Hall of Fame trainer + Hall of Fame (or HOF-caliber) jockey combinations:
- Derby: Mott + Alvarado on Sovereignty
- Preakness: Brown + Irad Ortiz Jr. on Journalism (Mott/Alvarado finished 2nd)
- BC Classic: Pletcher + Velazquez on Fierceness
**The rule:** Treat HOF trainer + HOF jockey as its own tier in the analysis, not as a tie-breaker. These combinations know how to peak a horse for the biggest races and have done it before. When a HOF combination is at 5-1 or shorter in a small field, the burden of proof shifts to finding a disqualifier — not finding a reason to bet them.

## Lesson 6 — Do Not Cite a Lesson and Then Ignore It
**Source:** 2025 Breeders' Cup Classic (Fierceness won at 5-2 after Heshie cited Lesson 2 and still gave the favorite zero win money)
**The miss:** In the Classic write-up Heshie literally referenced Lesson 2 by name ("don't blindly fade small-field favorites") and then assigned Fierceness no win-bet capital. The lesson was named in the analysis but not reflected in the ticket structure. Fierceness won.
**The rule:** If a lesson is cited in the written analysis, the bet structure must reflect it. Naming a lesson without acting on it is a worse process failure than not knowing the lesson at all — it means the rules are decorative, not operational. Before submitting any ticket: re-read the analysis, identify every lesson cited, and verify each one is reflected in the bet structure. If a lesson says "respect small-field favorites," then small-field favorites must receive non-zero win allocation unless a structural disqualifier is present.

## Lesson 7 — One Bad Race Is Not a Disqualifier When Class and Connections Are Intact
**Source:** 2025 Breeders' Cup Classic (Fierceness won despite a known Derby flop on his record)
**The miss:** Heshie used Fierceness's Derby flop as a primary reason to downgrade him, even though his class (HOF connections, multiple stakes wins, BC Juvenile winner) was intact and he was at a fair small-field price.
**The rule:** A single bad race in an otherwise strong form cycle is a yellow flag, not a red one. To downgrade a horse from a contender, the fade reason must be **structural** — surface change without pedigree, layoff over 180 days with cold trainer, declining speed figures across three or more consecutive races, distance question with negative pedigree, post-position disqualifier — not narrative ("I don't trust him" / "he flopped once"). Form cycles bend. Class does not.

## Lesson 8 — Pedigree Can Override Post Position When Pace Scenario Aligns
**Source:** 2026 Kentucky Derby (Golden Tempo won from Post 19 at 40-1)
**The miss:** Heshie correctly identified Golden Tempo's pedigree ("Curlin x Bernardini is a stamina pedigree built for this distance") and then overrode that read entirely based on Post 19. Golden Tempo won the race. The Curlin x Bernardini stamina combination in a fast-pace scenario was the decisive factor — the horse closed from the clouds exactly as the pedigree suggested.
**The rule:** Post position is a strong tendency, not an absolute disqualifier. When a horse has (1) a specific stamina pedigree that fits the distance, (2) a closing style that benefits from a fast pace, AND (3) multiple speed horses in the field likely to create that fast pace — the post position penalty must be reduced significantly. A closing-style, stamina-bred horse is less hurt by an outside post than a speed horse. Do not let post position kill a horse whose pedigree and pace scenario are both pointing toward a big run.

## Lesson 9 — If a Horse Is in the Trifecta, They Belong in the Exacta
**Source:** 2026 Kentucky Derby (Golden Tempo won, Renegade 2nd — exacta 19-1 paid a fortune)
**The miss:** Heshie included Golden Tempo in the trifecta box but not the exacta box. Golden Tempo won and Renegade ran 2nd. The exacta was the biggest single payout of the race and it was not covered.
**The rule:** If a horse makes the trifecta box, they must also be in the exacta box. The logic is simple — if you believe a horse can finish in the top 3, they can finish in the top 2. Leaving a horse in the trifecta but out of the exacta is an internal contradiction. The only exception is a horse you are specifically including as a 3rd-place-only longshot with no realistic path to 2nd — and that is rare. Default rule: trifecta inclusion = exacta inclusion.

## Lesson 10 — Always Use a Superfecta in the Kentucky Derby
**Source:** 2026 Kentucky Derby (Ocelli 99-1 ran 3rd, Post 22, added Thursday)
**The miss:** The app did not allow a superfecta bet and Heshie accepted that without pushing for an alternative. Ocelli — graded F, added Thursday, Post 22, 99-1 — finished 3rd. The superfecta 19-1-22-? would have paid life-changing money.
**The rule:** The Kentucky Derby superfecta is non-negotiable. Use a different platform, a different app, or a physical ticket at the track if necessary — but always have a $0.10 superfecta in the Kentucky Derby. The field size, the chaos, and the historical frequency of extreme longshots hitting the board make the superfecta the highest expected-value bet on the card. A $12 superfecta box (5 horses at $0.10) covering your top 3 plus 2 extreme longshots is mandatory. Never skip it.

## Lesson 11 — When Experts Pick a Horse You Respect But Fade on Structure, Put Them in the Exacta
**Source:** 2026 Kentucky Derby (TwinSpires expert picked Golden Tempo at 40-1, Heshie disagreed on win but respected the pedigree)
**The miss:** Heshie acknowledged Golden Tempo's pedigree was "real" and noted the expert backing, then faded the horse for the win AND left him out of the exacta. The expert was right. The pedigree was right. Heshie was right about the angle and wrong about the bet structure.
**The rule:** When an expert pick and your own pedigree/pace read both point toward a horse — even if you disagree on the win — that horse must be in the exacta box. Expert backing plus your own identified angle is a double confirmation. That is exactly when the exacta provides value: you are not betting the horse to win, you are betting that the angle is real enough to get them into the top two. At 40-1 the exacta payout justifies the coverage many times over.
