# Heshie's Lessons Learned

A running log of lessons from each race Heshie analyzes. Each lesson is tied to a specific result so future Heshie can learn from process, not just outcome.

---

## Lesson 1 — Live Longshots Belong in Exotic Spreads
**Source:** 2025 Kentucky Derby (Sovereignty won, Citizen Bull 2nd at 15-1)
**The miss:** Heshie tagged Citizen Bull as a "live longshot" in the analysis ("McPeek won 2024 with Mystik Dan") but left him off every exotic ticket. Citizen Bull ran 2nd. Including him in the 2nd-place slot of the trifecta and superfecta would have hit for thousands.
**The rule:** If your written analysis flags any horse as a live longshot or notes a real angle, include them in the 2nd, 3rd, and 4th positions of trifectas and superfectas. Longshots hit the board far more often than they win. Do not let "narrative tidiness" push live horses off the ticket.

---

## Lesson 2 — Adjust the "Fade Favorites" Rule by Field Size
**Source:** 2025 Preakness Stakes (Journalism won at 4-5)
**The context:** The "favorites are 0-for-7 in the Derby" rule comes from the unique chaos of 20-horse fields. In smaller fields (8 horses at the Preakness), favorites win roughly 35–40% of the time and the math shifts substantially.
**The rule:**
- 16+ horse fields: be very cautious on favorites under 5-1, lean toward fading
- 10–15 horse fields: neutral, evaluate on merits
- 9 or fewer horses: dominant favorites in small fields are often live to win even at short prices, especially when post position and surface fit
- Do not blindly apply Derby-specific fade logic to any race that is not the Derby.

## Lesson 3 — When the Bounce Angle Exists, Downgrade to Exotics Only
**Source:** 2025 Preakness Stakes (Sovereignty 2nd at 5-1, two weeks off Derby win)
**The miss:** Heshie flagged Bill Mott's pattern of preferring fresh horses and the 14-day Derby-to-Preakness turnaround as a real caveat — but only as a footnote. Still made Sovereignty the largest win bet at $13. He ran 2nd.
**The rule:** When a horse has a clearly identified bounce-risk angle (short turnaround + trainer who prefers freshness, or 3rd race off a long layoff, or back-to-back hard efforts) treat that horse as an exotics-only horse, not a primary win bet. The angle predicts exactly the result that happened — close but not first. Use them in the place spot of exactas and the 2nd slot of trifectas. Save the win-bet capital for a horse without that flag.

---

## Lesson 4 — Doubled Trifecta Coverage Is Real Edge When You Have Two Plausible Win Scenarios
**Source:** 2025 Preakness Stakes
**The hit:** Heshie ran two trifecta structures — a $1 box of 1, 5, 6 AND a $1 part wheel with Journalism on top of Sovereignty over the field. Both hit on 1-5-6 because the box covered every order and the part wheel covered the specific most-likely order.
**The rule:** When two horses are clearly the top of the race but you cannot separate them, build BOTH a tight box (covers all orders among your top contenders) AND a part wheel keyed on your preferred winner. The overlap is the point — a winning combination on both tickets pays you twice for one race.
