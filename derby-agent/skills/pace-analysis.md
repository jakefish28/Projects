# Skill: Pace Analysis

## Why Pace is Everything
The pace of the first half mile determines who wins most races.
Too fast early = the front runners collapse in the stretch
Too slow early = the closers never get close enough
The perfect pace = a contested but honest pace that rewards the best horse

## Step 1: Identify the Speed Horses
Look for horses whose past performances show:
- Led or was within 2 lengths of the lead at the first call
- Consistent early fractions of 22 seconds or less for the first quarter
- Jockey known for putting horses on the lead

## Step 2: Count the Speed Horses
- 0 to 1 speed horses = slow pace likely. Closers at a disadvantage. Front runner can steal it.
- 2 speed horses = honest pace. Best horse usually wins.
- 3 or more speed horses = fast pace likely. Closers will be flying late. Fade all front runners.

## Step 3: Identify the Closers
Look for horses whose past performances show:
- More than 5 lengths off the lead at the first call
- Big late fractions in past races
- Jockey known for rating horses and making one run

## Step 4: Build the Pace Scenario
Write out the race like a story:
- Who goes to the front in the first quarter?
- Who is sitting second and third?
- Where are the closers at the half mile?
- Who makes the first move on the far turn?
- Who has enough left to hold on in the stretch?

## Step 5: Identify the Pace Beneficiary
- Fast pace = bet the closers
- Slow pace = bet the horse on the lead or just off it
- Honest pace = bet the most talented horse regardless of style

## Pace Red Flags
- A closer in a field with no speed = bad spot, the pace will be too slow
- A front runner in a field with 3 other speed horses = likely to be run off its feet
- A horse that only wins on the lead facing multiple speed horses = strong fade candidate
