# Skill: How to Build a Betting Ticket

## The Golden Rule
Always match the bet type to your confidence level.
High confidence = win bets. Medium confidence = exactas and trifectas. 
Low confidence = superfectas and longshot plays.

## Straight Bets

### Win
Bet on a horse to finish first.
Best value in the Derby. If your horse wins, you collect.
Only use when you have a strong conviction AND the odds are 6-1 or better.
Never bet the favorite to win. Favorites are 0 for 7 in the last 7 Derbies.

### Place
Bet on a horse to finish first OR second.
Pays less than a win bet but gives you two chances to cash.
Use when you love a horse but worry about a tough post or traffic.

### Show
Bet on a horse to finish first, second, OR third.
Lowest payout of the straight bets but safest.
Good for longshots you think can hit the board without winning.

### Across the Board
One bet that covers win, place, and show simultaneously.
If you bet $2 across the board that is actually three $2 bets totaling $6.
Use on your single strongest horse of the day.

## Exotic Bets

### Exacta
Pick the first AND second place finisher in exact order.
Example: 12-6 means Chief Wallabee wins, Commandment second.
A $2 exacta on one combination costs $2.
BOX an exacta if unsure of order: $2 exacta box 12 and 6 = $4 total, covers 12-6 and 6-12.
Best value exotic for the Derby. High payouts, manageable cost.

### Trifecta
Pick first, second, AND third in exact order.
A $1 straight trifecta on one combination costs $1.
BOX options:
- 3 horse box at $1 = $6 total
- 4 horse box at $1 = $24 total
- 5 horse box at $1 = $60 total
PART WHEEL: Put one horse on top, box the rest underneath.
Example: 12 on top, 1/6/9/18 underneath for second and third = cheaper than full box.
This is the most popular Derby bet for serious handicappers.

### Superfecta
Pick first, second, third, AND fourth in exact order.
Massive payouts. The Derby superfecta often pays five figures.
Always bet superfectas at $0.10 base, not $1.
A $0.10 superfecta box with 5 horses = $12 total.
A $0.10 superfecta box with 6 horses = $36 total.
Use your top 3 horses plus 2 longshots to keep costs down.

### Pick 3
Pick the winners of 3 consecutive races.
Not just for the Derby. Use for the undercard races leading up to it.
Minimum bet is usually $0.50 per combination.
Good way to build bankroll before the main event.

### Pick 4 and Pick 5
Same as Pick 3 but across 4 or 5 races.
Higher risk, much higher reward.
Only attempt if you have strong opinions on multiple races.

### Daily Double
Pick the winners of 2 consecutive races.
Often offered for the last two races of the day.
Good beginner exotic. Simple and cheap.

## Value Rules

### What is Value?
Value means the odds on a horse are BETTER than its true chance of winning.
A horse with a 1 in 8 chance of winning should be 7-1.
If that horse is 11-1, that is value. Bet it.
If that horse is 4-1, it is overlaid. Skip it.

### How to Spot Value in the Derby
- Horses trained by elite trainers at lower than expected odds = overlaid, skip
- Horses with winning connections (same trainer and jockey as recent Derby winner) at double digit odds = value
- Japanese horses are often underestimated by American bettors = potential value
- Horses from post 17 or higher almost never win = avoid in win bets regardless of odds

### Bankroll Management
Never bet more than 10 percent of your total bankroll on a single race.
Split your Derby budget like this:
- 40 percent on your strongest straight bet
- 40 percent on trifectas and exactas
- 20 percent on superfectas and longshot plays
