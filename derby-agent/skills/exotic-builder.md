# Skill: Advanced Exotic Ticket Construction

## The Core Principle
Exotic bets are where real money is made in horse racing.
A $2 win bet on a 10-1 shot returns $22.
A $1 trifecta with that same horse on top can return hundreds or thousands.
The goal is to structure tickets that are cheap to play but cover enough combinations to cash.

## The Live Longshot Rule (Lesson from 2025 Derby)
If your analysis flags a horse as a "live longshot" or notes a real angle on them — even a C-tier horse — include them in your exotic spreads. Especially in the 2nd, 3rd, and 4th positions of trifectas and superfectas.

In the 2025 Kentucky Derby, Heshie tagged Citizen Bull (#10) as a live longshot in the analysis ("McPeek won this race in 2024 with Mystik Dan") but left him off every exotic ticket. Citizen Bull finished 2nd. Including him in the 2nd-place slot would have hit the trifecta and likely the superfecta for thousands.

The lesson: longshots hit the board far more often than they win. If you've already done the work to identify a live one, use them in the spread — that is where the value lives. Do not let "narrative tidiness" push live horses off the ticket.

## Exacta Construction

### Basic Exacta
Pick horse A on top of horse B.
Use when you are very confident in the exact order.
$2 exacta on 6 over 12 = $2 cost.

### Exacta Box
Use when confident in two horses but unsure of order.
$2 exacta box 6 and 12 = $4 cost (covers 6-12 and 12-6).

### Exacta Wheel
Put one horse on top, spread underneath.
$1 exacta wheel 6 on top of 1,9,12,18 = $4 cost.
Use when very confident in the winner but unsure of second place.

## Trifecta Construction

### Straight Trifecta
Pick exact order of first, second, third.
Only use when extremely confident.

### Trifecta Box
Three horse box at $1 = $6
Four horse box at $1 = $24
Five horse box at $1 = $60
Use when confident in three to five horses but unsure of order.

### Part Wheel
Single a horse on top, box the rest for second and third.
Example: 6 on top, 1,9,12,18 for second and third.
Cost: $1 part wheel with 4 horses underneath = $12.
This is the most efficient trifecta structure for value.

### The Key Horse Approach
When you have one horse you love and want to maximize return:
- Key it on top of a trifecta part wheel
- Use 4 to 6 horses underneath
- Keep the cost under $20

## Superfecta Construction

### Always Bet Superfectas at $0.10
A $0.10 superfecta box with 5 horses = $12
A $0.10 superfecta box with 6 horses = $36
Never bet superfectas at $1 base. Too expensive.

### Superfecta Strategy
Take your top 3 horses plus 2 to 3 longshots.
Longshots hit the superfecta far more often than they win.
A 30-1 shot finishing fourth is common. Use them in your fourth position.

## Pick 3, Pick 4, Pick 5 Construction

### When to Play Multi Race Bets
Only when you have strong opinions on all races involved.
Passing one race is better than guessing in a Pick 4.

### Singling a Horse
When you are very confident in one race use a single (one horse) in that leg.
This reduces cost dramatically and forces the payout higher.
Example: Pick 4 with singles in races 1 and 3, two horses in race 2, three horses in race 4.
Cost: 1 x 2 x 1 x 3 = 6 combinations at $0.50 = $3 total.

### Spreading in Contentious Races
When you cannot separate the field use 3 to 4 horses in that leg.
Never use more than 5 horses in a single leg or the ticket becomes too expensive.

### The 20 Percent Rule
Never spend more than 20 percent of your daily bankroll on a single multi race ticket.
If your bankroll for the day is $100, no single Pick 4 ticket should cost more than $20.

## The Kentucky Derby Exotic Strategy
Race 1 through Race 11 are the undercard.
The Derby is Race 12.
The best multi race bet on Derby day is the Pick 5 ending with the Derby.
Single your best horse in the Derby leg to keep costs manageable.
Spread in the undercard legs where fields are less familiar.
