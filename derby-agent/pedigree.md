# Pedigree Analysis Guide

## Why Pedigree Matters
Pedigree tells you what a horse is built for before it ever runs.
A horse bred for speed may not stay a mile and a quarter.
A horse bred for stamina may be slow early but finish like a freight train.

## Key Sire Lines for the Kentucky Derby

### Stamina Sires (love the mile and a quarter)
- Tapit — produces horses that love distance, often slow starters
- Curlin — tough, durable, loves Churchill Downs specifically
- Gun Runner — stamina and class, excellent Derby sire
- Medaglia d'Oro — consistent producer of distance horses
- Into Mischief — versatile, produces horses that handle any distance

### Speed Sires (may not stay the distance)
- Nyquist — speed heavy, question mark at a mile and a quarter
- American Pharoah — produces talented horses but mixed Derby results
- Justify — still building his book, early results promising

### Mud Sires (horses that love a wet track)
- Curlin — exceptional in mud
- Tapit — handles off tracks well
- Street Sense — wet track specialist

## How to Use Pedigree
Step 1: Look at the sire. Is it a stamina or speed sire?
Step 2: Look at the dam sire (mother's father). This tells you secondary influence.
Step 3: If both sire and dam sire suggest stamina, the horse is bred for the Derby distance.
Step 4: If there is a conflict between speed sire and stamina dam sire, the horse is a question mark.

## Pedigree Red Flags
- Two speed sires in the top four = may not stay
- No dirt pedigree on a horse moving from turf = serious concern
- First crop sire with no data yet = unknown quantity

## Pedigree Green Flags
- Tapit or Curlin anywhere in the first two generations = stamina
- Multiple Grade 1 winners in the dam line = class
- Full siblings that won at a mile or more = distance confirmed
