# Churchill Downs Track Biases

## About Churchill Downs
- Location: Louisville, Kentucky
- Track surface: Dirt (main track), Turf (course inside)
- Main track circumference: 1 mile
- Stretch length: 1,234 feet (one of the longest in America — favors closers)
- Typical Derby distance: 1 and a quarter miles (10 furlongs)

## Post Position History at Churchill Downs

### Sprint races (5 to 6 furlongs)
- Inside posts 1 through 4 are heavily favored
- Speed horses from inside posts dominate
- Outside posts face a pace disadvantage immediately

### Route races (1 mile and over)
- Posts 1 through 8 are favorable
- Posts 9 through 13 are neutral with a good trip
- Posts 14 and over require extra effort to find position
- In the Kentucky Derby specifically with 20 horses posts 17 and higher have extremely poor records

## How Track Conditions Affect Bias

### Fast track
- Speed horses and pressers are favored
- The rail is usually the best place to be
- Inside posts have a significant advantage

### Good track (slightly off)
- Slight advantage to off the pace horses
- Rail still good but less dominant

### Sloppy or muddy track
- Check pedigree for mud sires
- Closers gain a significant advantage
- Inside rail can become cuppy and slow — horses move off the rail
- Post position becomes less important

### Firm turf
- Outside posts less penalized on turf
- European horses often improve on firm turf
- Closers dominate on firm turf at Churchill

## The Churchill Downs Stretch
The long stretch at Churchill Downs is one of the most important factors in handicapping.
It gives late runners more time to close than at most tracks.
This means: do not dismiss closers just because they are far back at the half mile pole.
Horses that finish fast in the stretch at other tracks will finish even faster here.
